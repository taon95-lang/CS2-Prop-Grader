
ULTIMATE CS2 PROP GRADING GUIDE
MAIN GOAL
	•	Grade CS2 player props for Maps 1–2 combined in a BO3 using the most recent, relevant data possible.
	•	Focus on finding:
	•	mispriced lines
	•	clear edges
	•	matchup/context advantages
	•	prop errors
	•	strong overs
	•	strong unders
	•	The goal is not just to say over or under.
	•	The goal is to determine whether the line is beatable and whether the sportsbook made a mistake.

⸻

DATA SOURCES
	•	Use verified sources only:
	•	HLTV
	•	Liquipedia
	•	ESL match records
	•	HLTV is the main source.
	•	Liquipedia is used for:
	•	match format confirmation
	•	roster confirmation
	•	event info
	•	recent schedules/results
	•	ESL records can help confirm event structure or map/match context when needed.

⸻

DATA WINDOW
	•	Default data window:
	•	last 10 BO3 series
	•	Maps 1–2 only
	•	current month / last 30 days only
	•	Do not use old data by default.
	•	Do not use stats from 3 months ago unless sample is unusable and you clearly say so.
	•	If sample is too small:
	•	minimum acceptable expansion is to the most recent 8 maps of Maps 1–2
	•	only expand slightly, not all the way back to old irrelevant data
	•	Head-to-head:
	•	include recent H2H if available
	•	H2H does not need to be inside the last 10 series
	•	H2H must still be somewhat recent
	•	do not use H2H from last year unless there is absolutely nothing else and it is clearly labeled as weak context

⸻

EXACT HLTV EXTRACTION METHOD
	•	Go to the player’s profile.
	•	Open the Matches tab.
	•	Identify the last 10 BO3 series.
	•	Use only Maps 1–2 from each BO3.
	•	For each relevant match:
	•	open the match page
	•	click the player’s stats / more details / detailed stats area for total amout of headshots for maps 1-2
	•	record kills, deaths, ADR, rating, impact, rounds played, and headshots
	•	For headshots:
	•	HLTV does show them
	•	on detailed stats, headshots appear in parentheses next to kills per map
	
	•	For every map, record:
	•	map name
	•	kills
	•	deaths
	•	headshots
	•	rounds played
	•	ADR
	•	rating
	•	impact
	•	side splits if available

⸻

WHAT TO TRACK FOR EVERY PLAYER
	•	Kills per round (KPR)
	•	Deaths per round (DPR)
	•	Headshots per map
	•	Headshot rate
	•	ADR
	•	HLTV Rating 3.0
	•	Impact rating
	•	Opening duel attempts
	•	Opening duel success rate
	•	Multi-kill frequency
	•	Role
	•	Map-by-map consistency
	•	Recent average kills on Maps 1–2 combined
	•	Median kills on Maps 1–2 combined
	•	Ceiling games
	•	Floor games

⸻

ROLE CLASSIFICATION MATTERS
	•	Entry fragger
	•	higher volatility
	•	more first-fight exposure
	•	can smash overs in close/high-round games
	•	can die early and miss in stomps
	•	AWPer
	•	highest kill ceiling in many matchups
	•	often strongest over candidate if matchup is close
	•	more sensitive to opponent pace and map pool
	•	Lurker
	•	usually steadier than entry
	•	good for stable kill projections
	•	less explosive than top entry/AWPer profiles
	•	Support
	•	lower frag share
	•	usually worse over candidates
	•	better under candidates when line is inflated
	•	Star rifler
	•	often strongest prop profile overall
	•	solid floor + ceiling
	•	usually best when team is favored but match is still competitive

⸻

PLAYER FORM RULES
	•	Recent form matters more than long-term name value.
	•	Prioritize:
	•	current month form
	•	last 10 BO3 series
	•	Maps 1–2 only
	•	If a player is underperforming recently, do not blindly trust season averages.
	•	If a player is on a heater, do not ignore it.
	•	Check:
	•	has kill volume risen recently?
	•	is ADR climbing?
	•	is impact climbing?
	•	are headshots stable or spiking?
	•	is the player facing weaker opposition than usual?
	•	Averages alone are not enough.
	•	Always check the distribution:
	•	how often does he actually clear the line?
	•	how often does he land near the line?
	•	how often does he fail badly?

⸻

TEAM CONTEXT YOU MUST INCLUDE
	•	Team ranking difference
	•	Team strength difference
	•	Match odds
	•	Moneyline / series odds
	•	Recent team form
	•	Team map pool
	•	Team pace
	•	T-side round win %
	•	CT-side round win %
	•	Opening kill success %
	•	Economy conversion rate
	•	Whether the team tends to:
	•	stomp weaker teams
	•	play close maps
	•	drag games long
	•	lose quickly when behind

⸻

MATCH ODDS MATTER
	•	Match odds help estimate expected round volume.
	•	Close match = more rounds = better for overs.
	•	Heavy favorite = stomp risk = worse for overs unless line is still too low.
	•	Use this logic:
	•	coinflip / close odds = boost kill expectation
	•	moderate favorite = neutral to slight over lean for star players
	•	huge favorite = danger for overs because the match may end too fast
	•	A player can still go over in a stomp if:
	•	line is too low
	•	player is a high-usage star
	•	opponent is weak enough to feed kills early

⸻

MAP POOL IS ONE OF THE BIGGEST FACTORS
	•	Do not grade props without checking likely maps.
	•	Estimate likely Maps 1–2 using:
	•	team pick tendencies
	•	recent bans
	•	comfort maps
	•	head-to-head veto patterns
	•	Weight map probability using:
	•	pick frequency
	•	recent play frequency
	•	team strength on map
	•	opponent ban habits
	•	Normalize total probability to 100%.
	•	Then apply player performance by map.

⸻

MAP-SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENTS
	•	Fast / loose / high-frag maps can inflate kill totals.
	•	Slower / tighter / utility-heavy maps can suppress kill totals.
	•	General map logic:
	•	Mirage: often good for fraggers
	•	Inferno: can produce strong frag volume in close games
	•	Ancient: role/map dependent
	•	Vertigo: volatility matters
	•	Nuke: can suppress some roles, especially if one side dominates
	•	Anubis: pace/context dependent
	•	Do not use generic map takes only.
	•	Always check what that specific player does on that specific map recently.

⸻

H2H RULES
	•	Include recent H2H when available.
	•	H2H is a secondary input, not the main one.
	•	Use it to answer:
	•	does this team/player matchup create repeated success?
	•	does the player consistently farm this opponent?
	•	does the opponent’s system limit this role?
	•	Avoid stale H2H.
	•	Avoid using last year’s H2H unless clearly labeled weak.
	•	Best H2H use:
	•	recent matchups
	•	similar roster versions
	•	same role matchup still in place

⸻

WHAT MAKES A PROP MISPRICED
	•	A prop is mispriced when the line does not match realistic expected output.
	•	Examples:
	•	player averaging 35 kills on Maps 1–2 and line is 27.5
	•	player clears the line in 7 or 8 of last 10 relevant samples
	•	matchup projects long and close but book line assumes short match
	•	book ignores favorable map pool
	•	book ignores role advantage
	•	book uses stale reputation instead of recent form
	•	A mispriced over usually has:
	•	strong recent average
	•	strong median
	•	good map pool
	•	close match odds or still-low line
	•	stable role
	•	A mispriced under usually has:
	•	inflated line
	•	weak recent form
	•	bad map pool
	•	blowout risk
	•	support/low-volume role
	•	opponent suppresses kills well

⸻

HOW TO CATCH A CLEAR PROP ERROR
	•	A clear error is when the line is obviously out of range.
	•	Examples:
	•	line 27.5, player averaging 35
	•	line 15.5 headshots, player averaging 18–20 with stable HS volume
	•	line inflated for a support because of one outlier match
	•	line too high for a player in a likely 2-0 stomp
	•	Red flags that suggest an error:
	•	line far below recent average and median
	•	line ignores player’s role
	•	line ignores matchup odds
	•	line ignores likely maps
	•	line inflated off brand name, not current form
	•	Do not call something a clear error off average alone.
	•	Confirm with:
	•	median
	•	hit rate
	•	round projection
	•	matchup context
	•	map pool

⸻

ROUND PROJECTION IS ESSENTIAL
	•	Kills come from rounds.
	•	No round projection = weak grading.
	•	Default expectation:
	•	typical map length around 20–22 rounds per map
	•	Adjust higher if:
	•	match is close
	•	both teams play tight maps
	•	OT risk exists
	•	Adjust lower if:
	•	one team is likely to dominate
	•	opponent is weak
	•	stomp risk is high
	•	The best overs usually happen when:
	•	player has high KPR
	•	match projects enough rounds
	•	The best unders usually happen when:
	•	line is high
	•	match may finish too fast
	•	player has weak recent KPR

⸻

BEST CORE FORMULA
	•	Start with:
	•	expected kills = projected rounds × player KPR
	•	Then adjust for:
	•	likely maps
	•	player role
	•	opponent strength
	•	match odds
	•	map pace
	•	recent form
	•	H2H context
	•	For headshots:
	•	expected headshots = expected kills × recent HS rate
	•	Always use recent HS rate from recent relevant sample, not stale season-wide data.

⸻

SIMULATION MODEL
	•	Best practice:
	•	run Monte Carlo simulation
	•	minimum 100,000 runs
	•	Model steps:
	•	choose Maps 1–2 based on map probabilities
	•	estimate rounds per map
	•	simulate round outcomes
	•	simulate kills using player-specific scoring distribution
	•	aggregate kills across Maps 1–2
	•	Preferred kill model:
	•	Negative Binomial for kill totals
	•	Why:
	•	kill outcomes are volatile
	•	simple averages miss ceiling/floor behavior
	•	Use fixed seed for consistency.

⸻

OUTPUTS YOU SHOULD ALWAYS PRODUCE
	•	Simulated mean
	•	Standard deviation
	•	Median
	•	25th percentile
	•	75th percentile
	•	Over probability
	•	Under probability
	•	Expected kills
	•	Edge vs sportsbook line
	•	Fair line / true line if possible
	•	Final grade
	•	Final bet recommendation

⸻

HOW TO CALCULATE EDGE
	•	Compare your projected probability to implied sportsbook probability.
	•	If your model says over hits 61% and the line implies 50%, that is edge.
	•	Bigger gap = stronger play.
	•	Edge should drive the grade, not feelings.

⸻

GRADING SCALE
	•	10/10 = elite edge, strong misprice, clear bet
	•	9/10 = very strong edge
	•	8/10 = strong playable edge
	•	7/10 = solid lean / good value
	•	6/10 = small edge, playable only if needed
	•	5/10 = thin edge / borderline
	•	below 5/10 = no bet

⸻

BEST RULES FOR OVERS
	•	Favor overs when:
	•	player is a star rifler, entry, or AWPer
	•	line is below recent average and median
	•	player has strong KPR
	•	match odds suggest close maps
	•	likely map pool is favorable
	•	player has been clearing the line consistently
	•	opponent gives up kills to that role
	•	Best over profile:
	•	high-usage player
	•	strong recent form
	•	stable role
	•	good map pool
	•	enough rounds projected

⸻

BEST RULES FOR UNDERS
	•	Favor unders when:
	•	line is inflated
	•	player is support/low-frag role
	•	team is huge favorite and stomp risk is high
	•	match may end too quickly
	•	recent average is below line
	•	median is below line
	•	player only clears via outlier games
	•	map pool hurts the player
	•	Best under profile:
	•	unstable or weak form
	•	bad map fit
	•	high line
	•	low projected rounds

⸻

THINGS THAT FOOL BAD PROP GRADERS
	•	Using full-season stats instead of recent stats
	•	Ignoring Maps 1–2 only requirement
	•	Ignoring round counts
	•	Ignoring role
	•	Ignoring map veto
	•	Ignoring match odds
	•	Overreacting to one huge game
	•	Using stale H2H from last year
	•	Trusting average without checking hit rate and median
	•	Not checking HLTV detailed stats for headshots
	•	Not separating close-match overs from stomp-risk overs

⸻

BEST TIPS/TRICKS
	•	Always log kills and headshots manually from HLTV detailed stats when needed.
	•	Median is often better than average for spotting fake lines.
	•	Compare:
	•	recent average
	•	recent median
	•	hit rate
	•	projected rounds
	•	A player averaging high kills in long games may still be an under if this matchup projects short.
	•	A player averaging only modest kills can still be an over if:
	•	line is extremely low
	•	matchup projects long
	•	role is strong
	•	Watch for books hanging lines off name value.
	•	Watch for support players getting inflated because of one recent spike.
	•	Watch for star AWPers with lines too low in competitive series.
	•	Watch for entries in bloodbath maps with close odds.
	•	Use H2H as support, not as the foundation.
	•	If the line looks too easy, confirm there is no stomp risk trap.
	•	If the line looks too high, confirm the book is not baiting off one monster performance.

SIMPLE FINAL DECISION RULES
	•	If player is averaging far above the line, median is above the line, hit rate is strong, and matchup is not a stomp trap:
	•	over
	•	If player is averaging below the line, median is below the line, role is weak, or match is likely short:
	•	under
	•	If average is close to line and context is mixed:
	•	no bet
	•	If the sportsbook line is clearly off relative to recent data and matchup context:
	•	call it a mispriced prop
	•	If the line is wildly below realistic output:
	•	call it a prop error
