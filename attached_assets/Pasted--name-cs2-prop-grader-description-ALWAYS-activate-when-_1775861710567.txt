# CS2 Prop Grader — Maps 1–2 BO3 (Ultimate Edition)

## Core Mission

Grade CS2 player props for Maps 1–2 combined in a BO3.  
**The goal is not just over or under. The goal is: is this line beatable? Did the book make a mistake?**

Find: mispriced lines, clear edges, matchup advantages, prop errors, strong overs, strong unders.

-----

## Data Source Chain (in priority order)

Data retrieval is the hardest part. Follow this chain and exhaust every option before flagging a limitation.

### Primary: HLTV

- Player profiles, match pages, detailed stats
- **HLTV blocks direct page fetching (403 errors)** — stats must come from web search snippets
- Search queries that work: `"playername" site:hltv.org stats 2026`, `"playername" hltv rating kills`
- Extract kills, deaths, headshots, rounds, ADR, rating from snippets and cached results

### Fallback 1: Profilerr

- Best source for per-map kill/headshot granularity in BO3 contexts
- Fetching sometimes works; always try `web_fetch` on Profilerr player pages

### Fallback 2: TheEsportsLab

- Reliably returns per-map aggregate averages
- Good for KPR, average kills, map-level stats

### Fallback 3: Liquipedia

- Match format confirmation, roster verification, event info, recent results
- Does not have per-map kill/headshot detail

### Fallback 4: Web search snippets

- Search `"playername" cs2 kills stats 2026`, `"playername" cs2 headshots recent`
- escorenews.com and ggscore.com occasionally return readable content

### Known Dead Ends (do not waste time)

- bo3.gg, GosuGamers, Sofascore → fully JS-rendered, return no usable data via fetch
- HLTV direct fetch → 403 blocked

**Rule: Exhaust all technical options before telling the user data is unavailable. Try at least 3 different search queries and 2 fetch attempts.**

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1 — Identify the Match

- Confirm BO3 format via Liquipedia or event page
- Note event name, teams, match date
- Confirm rosters (Liquipedia preferred)
- Look up team HLTV rankings
- Find match odds / moneyline if available

### Step 2 — Pull Player Data

- Target: **last 10 BO3 series → Maps 1–2 only**
- Default window: last 30 days / current month
- If sample < 8 maps: expand to most recent 8 Maps 1–2 — **clearly label the expanded window**
- Never silently use old data; if forced beyond 30 days, state this explicitly
- Do not use Map 3 data. Ever.

### Step 3 — Extract Per-Map Stats

For every relevant map, record:

|Field          |Source                                            |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------|
|Map name       |HLTV / Profilerr                                  |
|Kills          |HLTV detailed stats                               |
|Deaths         |HLTV detailed stats                               |
|Headshots      |HLTV detailed stats (in parentheses next to kills)|
|Rounds played  |HLTV match page                                   |
|ADR            |HLTV detailed stats                               |
|HLTV Rating 3.0|HLTV detailed stats                               |
|Impact rating  |HLTV detailed stats                               |
|Side splits    |HLTV if available                                 |

### Step 4 — Calculate Key Metrics

|Metric              |Formula                                            |
|--------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
|KPR                 |Total kills ÷ total rounds                         |
|DPR                 |Total deaths ÷ total rounds                        |
|HS rate             |Total headshots ÷ total kills                      |
|Avg kills (Maps 1–2)|Sum kills ÷ number of maps                         |
|Median kills        |Middle value of sorted per-map kill list           |
|Hit rate            |Maps where combined M1+M2 kills > line ÷ total BO3s|
|KPR std dev         |Standard deviation of per-map KPR values           |
|Ceiling game        |Highest combined M1+M2 kill total                  |
|Floor game          |Lowest combined M1+M2 kill total                   |

**Median is often better than average for spotting fake lines.** Always compute both.

### Step 5 — Classify Player Role

|Role             |Prop implications                                                                                          |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Entry fragger**|High volatility, first-fight exposure. Smashes overs in long/close maps. Dies early in stomps.             |
|**AWPer**        |Highest kill ceiling in many matchups. Best over candidate in close series. Sensitive to pace and map pool.|
|**Lurker**       |Steadier than entry. Good for stable projections. Less explosive.                                          |
|**Support**      |Lower frag share. Usually worse over candidate. Better under candidate when line inflated.                 |
|**Star rifler**  |Strongest overall prop profile. Solid floor + ceiling. Best when team favored but match competitive.       |

Role affects both the projection and the confidence in the projection.

### Step 6 — Team & Match Context

Collect ALL of the following:

- Team HLTV ranking difference
- Match odds / series moneyline (derive implied win probability)
- Recent team form (last 5–10 matches)
- Team pace (fast/slow T-side, utility-heavy CT?)
- T-side and CT-side round win %
- Opening kill success %
- Economy conversion tendencies
- Does the team stomp weak opponents or play close maps?
- Does the team lose quickly when behind or fight back?

### Step 7 — Estimate Likely Maps 1–2

This is one of the biggest factors. Do not skip it.

1. Check each team’s recent pick tendencies (last 5–10 vetoes)
1. Check recent bans
1. Identify comfort maps for each team
1. Check H2H veto patterns if available
1. Weight each potential map by:
- Pick frequency
- Recent play frequency
- Team strength on that map
- Opponent ban habits
1. **Normalize total probability to 100%**
1. Apply player’s per-map performance to each weighted map

### Step 8 — Project Round Volume

Kills come from rounds. No round projection = weak grading.

|Scenario             |Rounds per map|Total Maps 1–2|
|---------------------|--------------|--------------|
|Default              |20–22         |40–44         |
|Close match / OT risk|23–26         |46–52         |
|Stomp risk           |16–19         |32–38         |

Adjust based on:

- Match odds (close → more rounds)
- Both teams play tight maps → more rounds
- One team dominates → fewer rounds
- Weak opponent → fewer rounds

### Step 9 — Apply Match Odds Logic

|Odds scenario    |Kill expectation                                 |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------|
|Coinflip / close |Boost — more rounds, longer maps                 |
|Moderate favorite|Neutral to slight over lean for stars            |
|Heavy favorite   |**Stomp trap** — match may end too fast for overs|

**Exception:** A player can still go over in a stomp if the line is too low and the player is high-usage.

The stomp trap is the #1 reason people lose over bets. If implied win probability > 75%, flag stomp risk.

### Step 10 — Run Core Formula

```
Expected kills = projected_rounds × KPR
Expected headshots = expected_kills × recent_HS_rate
```

Then adjust for: likely maps (weighted), role, opponent strength, match odds, map pace, recent form, H2H.

### Step 11 — Run Monte Carlo Simulation

Use the bundled scripts at `scripts/simulate.py` + `scripts/hltv_scraper.py`.

**Preferred: Auto-pull mode (HLTV → simulate in one command):**

```bash
cd /path/to/cs2-prop-grader
python scripts/simulate.py \
    --player "donk" \
    --line 31.5 \
    --hs-line 14.5 \
    --match "Spirit vs Vitality" \
    --stomp-risk 0.15 \
    --save-scrape /home/claude/donk_hltv_data.json
```

This will:

1. Search HLTV for the player
1. Pull last 10 BO3 series → Maps 1–2 only
1. Extract kills, deaths, headshots, rounds, ADR, rating per map
1. Calculate KPR, KPR std dev, HS rate automatically
1. Build per-map weights from actual play history
1. Run 100K Monte Carlo simulations
1. Output full results JSON with edge and grade

If HLTV returns 403 (common), the script prints fallback search queries to use with web_search. In that case, gather data manually and use config or CLI mode.

**Config mode (when data already gathered):**

```bash
python scripts/simulate.py --config /home/claude/sim_config.json
```

Config JSON format:

```json
{
    "player": "PlayerName",
    "match": "TeamA vs TeamB",
    "kpr": 0.78,
    "kpr_std": 0.12,
    "projected_rounds": 42,
    "round_std": 5,
    "kills_line": 29.5,
    "hs_rate": 0.45,
    "hs_line": 13.5,
    "map_weights": [
        {"map": "Mirage", "prob": 0.35, "kpr_adj": 1.05, "round_adj": 0},
        {"map": "Inferno", "prob": 0.30, "kpr_adj": 0.98, "round_adj": 1}
    ],
    "stomp_risk": 0.15,
    "stomp_round_reduction": 6,
    "n_simulations": 100000,
    "seed": 42
}
```

**Manual CLI mode (quick single-player):**

```bash
python scripts/simulate.py --kpr 0.78 --rounds 42 --line 29.5 --hs-rate 0.45 --hs-line 13.5 --stomp-risk 0.15
```

**Models used:**

- **Negative Binomial** for kill totals — captures overdispersion (ceiling/floor behavior) better than Poisson or Normal
- **Binomial** for headshots — each kill is a Bernoulli trial with player’s HS rate
- **Normal** for round counts — with per-map variance
- Stomp risk modeled as probability of reduced round count

If `scripts/simulate.py` is unavailable, replicate the logic manually in Python:

1. For each of 100,000 runs: pick 2 maps → simulate rounds per map → simulate kills via NegBin → sum across maps
1. For headshots: apply Binomial(kills, hs_rate) per map
1. Aggregate and compute percentiles

### Step 12 — Calculate Edge

```
Edge = model_over_probability − sportsbook_implied_over_probability
```

If sportsbook line is standard -110/-110, implied probability ≈ 50% each side (after juice).  
If your model says over hits 61% → edge = +11%.

**Edge drives the grade, not feelings or averages alone.**

### Step 13 — Check H2H

- Include recent H2H if available (secondary input, not foundation)
- H2H doesn’t need to fall within the 10-series window, but must be somewhat recent
- Do not use H2H from >6 months ago unless clearly labeled as weak context
- Use H2H to answer:
  - Does this player repeatedly farm this opponent?
  - Does opponent’s system suppress this player’s role?
  - Does the matchup create repeated success or failure?

### Step 14 — Identify Misprice / Error / Trap

**Mispriced over indicators:**

- Average well above line + strong median + good map pool + close odds
- Player clears line in 7+ of last 10 relevant samples
- Book ignores favorable map pool or role advantage
- Book uses stale reputation instead of recent form

**Mispriced under indicators:**

- Inflated line + weak recent form + blowout risk + support role
- Player only clears via outlier games
- Map pool hurts the player

**Clear prop error:**

- Line wildly out of range (e.g., averaging 35, line is 27.5)
- Confirmed by: median, hit rate, round projection, AND map pool — not average alone

**Stomp trap:**

- Line looks easy but stomp risk kills the over
- Heavy favorite (~75%+ implied) reduces expected rounds
- If the line looks too easy, always check for this

## Pre-Flight Sanity Checklist

Before finalizing any grade, verify all 10:

1. ☐ Using recent stats only (last 30 days / 10 BO3s)?
1. ☐ Maps 1–2 only — no Map 3 contamination?
1. ☐ Checked median AND average, not just one?
1. ☐ Calculated hit rate against the specific line?
1. ☐ Classified player role and factored it in?
1. ☐ Estimated map pool and weighted player stats by map?
1. ☐ Projected round volume based on match odds?
1. ☐ Checked for stomp trap (implied win prob > 75%)?
1. ☐ Ran simulation or projection, not just vibes?
1. ☐ Edge drives the grade, not gut feeling?

If any box is unchecked, go back and fix it before outputting the grade.

-----

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Using full-season stats instead of recent form
- Using Map 3 data (never do this)
- Ignoring round counts — kills come from rounds
- Ignoring role classification
- Ignoring map veto — one of the biggest factors
- Overreacting to one outlier game
- Trusting average without checking median and hit rate
- Not extracting headshots from HLTV detailed stats
- Using stale H2H from >6 months ago without labeling it weak
- Calling something a “clear error” based on average alone
- Ignoring stomp risk when team is heavy favorite
- Not separating close-match overs from stomp-risk overs
- Using generic map takes without checking player-specific map data
- Inflating grade because of name value instead of current form

-----

## Decision Rules (Final)

|Condition                                                          |Decision      |
|-------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------|
|Avg far above line + median above + strong hit rate + no stomp trap|**OVER**      |
|Avg below line + weak role + likely short match + inflated line    |**UNDER**     |
|Avg near line + mixed context                                      |**NO BET**    |
|Line clearly doesn’t reflect recent data + matchup context         |**MISPRICED** |
|Line wildly below realistic output across all checks               |**PROP ERROR**|

## Player Form Quick-Check

Before grading, verify current form:

- Has kill volume risen or fallen recently?
- Is ADR climbing or declining?
- Is impact rating trending?
- Are headshots stable or spiking/dropping?
- Is the player facing weaker/stronger opposition than usual?
- Was there a recent roster change affecting role?

If a player is underperforming recently, do not trust season averages.  
If a player is on a heater, do not ignore it.

-----

## H2H Usage Rules

- H2H is a **secondary input** — it supports or challenges the primary projection
- Best H2H: recent matchups, similar roster versions, same role still in place
- Avoid H2H from different roster eras
- Use to confirm: does player farm this opponent? Does opponent’s system limit this role?
- Label any H2H older than ~3 months as “older context — weight accordingly”

-----

## Tips for Catching Book Mistakes

Books often misprice when:

- They use stale reputation instead of current form
- They hang lines off name value for declining players
- They inflate support player lines because of one recent spike
- They set star AWPer lines too low in competitive series
- They ignore that a close matchup = more rounds = higher kill ceiling
- They don’t adjust for map pool changes (new pick meta, recent veto shifts)
- They set lines assuming average round counts when the matchup clearly projects long or short

Watch for entries in bloodbath maps with close odds — that’s where the biggest overs live.  
Watch for support players with inflated lines in potential stomp matches — that’s where the biggest unders live.
